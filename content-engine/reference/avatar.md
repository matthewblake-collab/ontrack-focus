# Target Audience — OnTrack Focus

Two tiers. Every piece of content should be written for one or both.

---

## MACRO — The Scroll-Stoppers

People scrolling who might follow. They don't know OnTrack exists yet. You need to earn their attention in 1.5 seconds.

**Demographics:**
- 22-35 year olds
- Male and female, skews slightly male
- Australia, US, UK primarily
- Active on Instagram Reels and TikTok
- Follow self-improvement, fitness, and habit content

**Psychographics:**
- Frustrated. They've tried to build habits before and quit.
- Sick of vague motivational content that says nothing specific
- Respond to "real talk" — data, honesty, direct language
- Want systems, not inspiration
- Distrust hustle culture but still want to be better
- Follow accounts that feel like smart friends, not coaches

**What they scroll past:**
- "Rise and grind" content
- Generic habit tips with no specifics
- Over-produced studio content that feels like an ad
- Anything that sounds like a LinkedIn post

**What stops their scroll:**
- Specific data ("I tracked every workout for 90 days")
- Calling out something they've experienced ("Here's why you quit every habit after 2 weeks")
- Fast app demos that show a result immediately
- Honest founder content that isn't a highlight reel

---

## MICRO — The Downloaders

People actively looking for a tracking app. They've probably tried one before. They're comparing options.

**Who they are:**
- Same demographic as macro, but further down the funnel
- Have tried Apple Health, Streaks, Habitica, or a generic habit app
- Found those apps lacking — too simple, too gamified, no social layer, no supplement tracking
- Care about: streaks, visual progress, data, clean UI, dark mode
- Will download a free app on the App Store if the pitch is specific enough

**What they're looking for:**
- An app that tracks habits AND supplements AND sessions in one place
- Group accountability — not just solo tracking
- Clean, dark aesthetic (not bright/gamified)
- Something that feels serious, not toy-like
- Real users or a real founder behind it — not a faceless startup

**What converts them:**
- Feature demos that show exactly what the app does
- Comparison content (OnTrack vs what they're currently using)
- User wins — real streak data, real consistency improvements
- Founder story — "built by someone who's been where I am"
- A clear, low-friction CTA — "Available now on iOS, link in bio"

---

## Audience Shorthand

When writing content, tag each piece with its target:

- **MACRO** — designed to attract new followers (hooks, education, controversy)
- **MICRO** — designed to convert followers into downloads (demos, comparisons, social proof)
- **BOTH** — works for both (founder story, user wins with app shown)

Most content should be MACRO (70%) to grow the audience. MICRO content (30%) converts the audience you've already built.
