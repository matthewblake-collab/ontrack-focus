# Scripting Voice — OnTrack Focus

How OnTrack sounds in every piece of content. Read this before writing anything.

---

## The Voice

A smart friend who's actually done the work. Not a coach. Not a guru. Not a brand.

Matt built this app because he lost his way and two mates pulled him back. That's the energy. Honest, specific, direct. The kind of person who gives you the real answer when you ask "what should I do?"

---

## Rules

### DO
- Use first person. "I" and "OnTrack" — never "we" or "the team"
- Lead with data and specifics: "People who track daily are 3x more likely to stick past 30 days"
- Use short sentences. One idea per sentence.
- Get to the point in the first line. No warm-up.
- Be occasionally self-aware: "Yeah, I built a habit tracking app. Ironic, I know."
- Use Australian English (behaviour, analyse, colour)
- Reference real features, real numbers, real experiences
- End with a CTA that feels earned, not forced

### DON'T
- Never use: journey, grind, hustle, crush it, level up, game changer, let's go, smash it, unlock your potential, take it to the next level, accountability partner (say "your group" instead)
- Never sound like a LinkedIn post
- Never open with "In today's world..." or "Have you ever..."
- Never use more than one emoji per post (zero is fine)
- Never use exclamation marks in hooks
- Never write paragraphs longer than 3 sentences
- Never explain what a habit is — the audience already knows
- Never be preachy. Stand beside the audience, not above them.

---

## Sentence Style

**Good:**
- "Most habit apps track what you did. None of them track whether you actually showed up."
- "Your supplement stack doesn't work because you forget by Wednesday."
- "I tracked every workout for 90 days. Here's what the data says."
- "Streaks work. But only if someone notices when yours breaks."

**Bad:**
- "In today's fast-paced world, building habits can be challenging."
- "Are you tired of starting and stopping your fitness routine?"
- "Let's dive into why consistency is the key to success!"
- "OnTrack Focus is a revolutionary app that will transform your daily habits."

---

## Hook Writing Rules

1. The hook is the first sentence. It does one job: stop the scroll.
2. No context before the hook. No "Hey guys" or "So I was thinking."
3. Hooks should create a gap — the viewer needs to know what comes next.
4. Use specifics: numbers, timeframes, named things.
5. Negative framing outperforms positive on short-form: "why you're failing" > "how to succeed"
6. Questions work if they're specific: "Why do 80% of people quit habits by day 14?" vs "Do you struggle with habits?"

---

## CTA Rules

- Always at the end, never at the start
- Match CTA to platform:
  - Instagram/TikTok: "Link in bio" or "Available now on iOS"
  - LinkedIn: Direct App Store URL acceptable
  - X/Threads: "Link in replies" or direct URL
- One CTA per post. Never stack CTAs.
- CTA should feel like a natural next step, not a sales pitch

---

## Example Script (30-second Reel)

**Hook (to camera):** "Most people don't fail at habits because they're lazy."

**Point 1:** "They fail because they track in their head instead of on a screen. You overestimate your consistency every single time."

**Point 2:** "I built OnTrack to fix that. One tap per habit. Your streak either grows or it doesn't. No lying to yourself."

**Point 3:** "Add your friends and they can see it too. That's the part that actually works."

**CTA:** "Available now on iOS. Link in bio."
