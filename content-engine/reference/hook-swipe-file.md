# Hook Swipe File — OnTrack Focus

15 proven hook templates organised by type. Use these as starting points — adapt with specific data, features, or angles for each piece of content.

Read `scripting-voice.md` before writing any hook. Every hook must pass: "Would I actually stop scrolling for this?"

---

## Curiosity Hooks

Create an information gap the viewer has to close.

**1. The Hidden Truth**
> "Most habit apps are lying to you about streaks."

Use when: exposing a misconception about tracking, fitness, or self-improvement.

**2. The Counter-Intuitive Claim**
> "The reason you can't stick to a habit has nothing to do with discipline."

Use when: challenging conventional wisdom. Must deliver a satisfying answer.

**3. The Insider Knowledge**
> "There's a feature in OnTrack that nobody talks about. It changed how I train."

Use when: revealing a specific app feature. Must show it within 5 seconds.

---

## Failure Hooks

Diagnose why things go wrong. High engagement because people want validation for their own failures.

**4. The Diagnosis**
> "Here's why you quit every habit after 2 weeks."

Use when: explaining a common failure pattern. Must offer a real fix, not just sympathy.

**5. The "You're Doing It Wrong"**
> "If you're tracking habits in Apple Health, you're making this mistake."

Use when: comparing OnTrack to a competitor or common approach. Be specific about the mistake.

**6. The Uncomfortable Truth**
> "Nobody's going to hold you accountable. That's the whole problem."

Use when: positioning group accountability as the missing piece. Works well with founder story angle.

---

## Demo Hooks

Show the app doing something impressive in the first 2 seconds.

**7. The Speed Run**
> "Watch me set up a full supplement stack in 30 seconds."

Use when: demonstrating how fast a feature works. Record at normal speed — don't fake it.

**8. The Reveal**
> "This is what 90 days of tracking actually looks like."

Use when: showing real data, streaks, or progress screens. Must use real data, not staged.

**9. The Side-by-Side**
> "Left: Apple Health. Right: OnTrack. They're not the same thing."

Use when: visual comparison. Keep it factual, show real differences, don't trash the competitor.

---

## Data Hooks

Lead with a number. Numbers stop the scroll.

**10. The Personal Experiment**
> "I tracked every workout for 90 days. Here's what actually changed."

Use when: sharing personal tracking data. Real numbers only — never fabricate.

**11. The Research Cite**
> "People who track habits daily are 3x more likely to maintain them past 30 days."

Use when: citing research or statistics. Link the data to why OnTrack helps.

**12. The Streak Flex**
> "Day 47. Not because I'm motivated. Because the streak won't let me stop."

Use when: showing streak psychology in action. Works as text overlay on a workout clip.

---

## Comparison Hooks

Position OnTrack against alternatives. Honest, not trashy.

**13. The "What I Switched From"**
> "I used Streaks for 6 months. Here's why I switched."

Use when: genuine comparison. Must show real differences, not just "mine is better."

**14. The Feature Gap**
> "Name one habit app that tracks supplements, sessions, AND has a group feed. I'll wait."

Use when: highlighting OnTrack's unique combination. Confident, not arrogant.

**15. The "They All Miss This"**
> "Every fitness app tracks what you do. None of them track whether your mates showed up too."

Use when: positioning the group accountability angle. The differentiator.

---

## Usage Notes

- Never use more than one hook per piece of content
- Test 3 hooks per video concept — pick the one with the strongest gap
- Negative hooks (4-6) consistently outperform positive hooks on TikTok and Reels
- Data hooks (10-12) perform best on LinkedIn and X
- Demo hooks (7-9) convert the most downloads (MICRO audience)
- Track which hook templates perform best in `content_posts.hook_type`
